Product case study · group project, 0→1 concept

Flow — a health companion built for your worst days, not your best.

A gentle, adaptive wellness app that replaces guilt-driven streaks with a design built around recovery — so missing a day never means starting over.

This was a group project built during an AI Product Management programme, with a four-person team. My role was leading user research and shaping the go-to-market strategy and pitch narrative. Where I describe "we," that's the full team's work; where I describe a decision or insight as mine, that's what I personally drove.

The problem

Riya has restarted her health routine more times than she can count.

Riya is 28, works in marketing, and knows the cycle by heart. She's motivated for a week. Life gets hard. She misses two days. The app's streak resets to zero, and the guilt of "starting over" feels worse than never trying at all. So eventually, she just stops opening the app.

Through the user research I led, we found this pattern everywhere: people don't abandon health routines because they stop caring. They abandon them because exhaustion and too many small decisions make even simple choices feel impossible on a hard day — and most apps are only designed for the good days.

"In this system, you can never fail. You can only Flow."

The decision

Measure return, not consistency.

Most health apps reward discipline with streaks — and punish exactly the moment someone is already struggling by breaking that streak visually. Flow's core decision was to flip what gets measured entirely.

Most health apps
Streak breaks the moment you miss a day. Progress resets to zero. The visual punishment lands exactly when someone is already struggling most.
→
Flow
Momentum never fully resets. A hard day dips it slightly — coming back the next day is what the whole system is designed to notice and reward.
How it works

Four ideas, working together quietly in the background.

None of these are flashy on their own — together, they're what makes the app feel like it's adapting to a person rather than judging them.

Passive intelligence
Reads quiet signals — how late someone's checking in, how often they're opening the app — to understand energy level without ever having to ask directly.
Adaptive support states
The whole interface shifts based on energy — fewer choices and gentler suggestions on a drained day, more options on a good one.
Decision fatigue reduction
Fewer, better choices instead of an overwhelming menu — a 5-minute meal idea shown instead of fifty options to scroll through.
Recovery instead of punishment
Built to make returning after a break feel easy and welcome, not like starting over from scratch.
The signature feature

The Anchor — what happens after 48 hours away.

This is the moment most apps get wrong — guilt, a broken streak, a wall of missed days. Flow does the opposite.

Why it matters

The 48 hours away never erase what came before. Momentum dips, but it doesn't zero out — so coming back never feels like undoing everything that was already built.

Reflection

What the whole team took away from building this.

Shared honestly, exactly as the team discussed it after the project wrapped.

Key lessons learned
  • Emotional exhaustion is the real barrier, not lack of fitness knowledge.
  • Small wins count and encourage better recovery.
  • Today's apps aren't designed for real life interruptions.
  • Reducing cognitive load is a major trait users need.
Challenges encountered
  • Translating emotional patterns into actual product flows.
  • Balancing meaningful support without therapeutic boundaries.
  • Maintaining a careful tone without sounding clinical.
  • Avoiding feature creep while communicating the full vision.
What we'd do differently
  • Narrow MVP scope earlier, focus on the Anchor experience.
  • Conduct more real user testing during early prototyping.
  • Simplify screens further to reduce cognitive overload.
  • Prioritise core recovery flows over multiple features.
Future improvements
  • AI-powered adaptive recommendations by energy and behaviour.
  • Passive intelligence: stress and burnout detection.
  • A dedicated "Worst-Day Mode" with ultra low-effort support.
  • Expand into sleep, hydration, and stress management.
